My projects


I have created several projects over the years, so here's the ones I am most proud of. Many of these focus on researching and documenting rather niche, nerdy topics, but ultimately I feel I have done a lot of good things for them. Another reason I tend to work on more niche projects is because I like to "fill in the gaps"; documenting stuff no one else has bothered to do before.

Ringtone Bangers


I run a Twitter account called Ringtone Bangers, which posts two great ringtones a day and one piece of tech-related music a week. I created this as I feel many ringtones are underappreciated and are legitimately good pieces of music, rather than just being "good for a ringtone". The account has managed to gain a large cult following of over 20K+ followers, with several ringtones gaining thousands of likes. If this account sounds like your cup of tea, go follow it!

Asides from running that account, I am legitimately interested in ringtones and who made them/how they were made. I am working on a few spreadsheets documenting ringtones, and contacting original composers for more information.

Wallpaper projects


I have also created many projects related to Windows wallpapers, which was a hyperfixation of mine for a few years. These include: packs featuring higher resolution versions of wallpapers, a wiki dedicated to this subject, and a spreadsheet where I document my finds. I feel I have excelled with my work as I have been able to spread high resolution wallpapers and previously undiscovered information.

  • Windows XP HD Wallpaper Pack
  • Windows Vista and 7 HD Wallpaper Pack
  • Windows HD User Account Picture Pack
  • Windows Wallpaper Wiki
  • The original files of some Windows wallpapers
  • A spreadsheet detailing Windows wallpaper origins
